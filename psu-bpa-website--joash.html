About the Program

Preparing Tomorrow's
Public Leaders

The Bachelor of Public Administration (BPA) program at Pangasinan State University develops students into competent public servants, ethical leaders, and community-oriented professionals capable of contributing to governance and nation-building.

Through a comprehensive curriculum that blends theory and practice, students gain hands-on experience in public management, policy analysis, and community development — preparing them for impactful careers in government, NGOs, and the public sector.

"The BPA program is your gateway to a life of meaningful public service — where your work creates lasting impact for communities, institutions, and the Filipino people."

Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions?
We Have Answers.

What is the Bachelor of Public Administration program?
The BPA is a four-year degree program that prepares students for careers in public service, governance, and administration. It covers public management, policy analysis, leadership, ethics, and community development to produce competent and ethical public servants.
What are the admission requirements for BPA?
Applicants must be senior high school graduates or holders of an equivalent qualification. Requirements typically include: high school diploma or Form 138, PSU Entrance Examination results, birth certificate, and other documents as required by the University Registrar. Please contact the PSU Lingayen campus for the most current requirements.
Are scholarships available for BPA students?
Yes! PSU offers various scholarship programs including CHED UniFAST grants, LEDVFI scholarships, and institutional scholarships for deserving and underprivileged students. The Free Higher Education Act also covers tuition and other school fees for qualified students at state universities like PSU.
What jobs can I get after graduating from BPA?
BPA graduates are well-prepared for a wide range of public sector careers including positions as public administrators, planning officers, administrative officers, HR managers, project leaders, and development managers. You can work in national government agencies, local government units (LGUs), NGOs, international organizations, and even the private sector.
Where is PSU's BPA program located?
The BPA program is offered at Pangasinan State University, located in Lingayen, Pangasinan, Philippines — the provincial capital. The main campus provides excellent facilities, a modern library, computer labs, and a supportive academic community to ensure the best learning experience.
Can I pursue graduate studies after BPA?
Absolutely! BPA graduates can pursue a Master of Public Administration (MPA), Master of Public Management, or other graduate programs in law, business, or social sciences. A graduate degree significantly enhances your career advancement prospects in government and public service.
